# Class Features

As a mystic, you gain the following class features.

##### Hit Points

**Hit Dice:** 1d8 per mystic level  
**Hit Points at 1st Level:** 8 + your Constitution modifier  
**Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per mystic level after 1st

##### Proficiencies

**Armor:** Light armor  
**Weapons:** Simple weapons  
**Tools:** None  
**Saving Throws:** Intelligence, Wisdom  
**Skills:** Choose two skills from Arcana, History, Insight, Medicine, Nature, Perception, and Religion

##### Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

- (a) a spear or (b) a mace
- (a) leather armor or (b) studded leather armor
- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) any simple weapon
- (a) a scholar’s pack or (b) an explorer’s pack

Alternatively, you can ignore the equipment here and in your background, and buy 5d4 × 10 gp worth of equipment from chapter 5 in the Player’s Handbook.

## Psionic Power

At 1st level, you awaken within yourself your psionic potential. This psionic potential is represented by your Psionic Talent die, the starting size of which is a d6. Your Psionic Talent die is used for a number of Psionic Talents.

If an ability dictates that you roll your Psionic Talent die and you roll the highest value, your Psionic Talent die reduced by one size. This represents you burning through your psionic reserves. For example, if the die is a d6 and your roll a 6, it becomes a d4. If it's a d4 and you roll a 4, it becomes unusable until you finish a long rest or an ability increases the size of your Psionic Talent die.

Conversely, if you roll a 1 on your Psionic Talent die, it increases by one die size after the roll, up to its starting size. This represents a conservation of psionic energy for later use. For example, if you roll a 1 on a d4, the die then becomes d6.

In addition to rolls having the potential to shrink or increase the size of your Psionic Talent die, certain abilities may dictate that you automatically increase or decrease the size of your Psionic Talent die. For example, if an ability dictated that you decrease the size of your Psionic Talent die while it is a d4, it becomes unusable. Conversely, if your Psionic Talent die is currently unusable and an ability dictates that you increase the size of your Psionic Talent die, your Psionic Talent die is returned as a d4.

Whenever you finish a long rest, your Psionic Talent die resets to its starting size. When you reach certain levels in this class, the starting size of your Psionic Talent die increases: at 5th level (d8), 11th level (d10), and 17th level (d12). Psi Replenishment. As a bonus action, you can calm your mind for a moment and restore your Psionic Talent die to its starting size. You then can’t use Psi Replenishment again until you finish a long rest.

## Psionic Capacity

While some psionic abilities are powered by only one faculty of the mind, others call on the totality of your conscious might. Accordingly, your total Psionic Potential can be determines as follows.

Psionic Capacity = Intelligence modifier + Wisdom modifier + Charisma modifier

## Pneuma

Starting at 2nd level, you become attuned to the subtle flow of energy within yourself. Your access to this force is represented by a number of pneuma points. Your mystic level determines the number of points you have, as shown in the Pneuma Points column of the Mystic table.

You can use the points to augment your various psionic powers.

When you spend a pneuma point, it is unavailable until you finish a short or long rest, at the end of which you recollect all of your expended pneuma within yourself. You must spend at least 30 minutes of your rest meditating to recollect your pneuma.

**Two Words, One Essence.** Pneuma is interchangeable with Ki.

You have access to the following Pneuma abilities at this level.

**Ebb and Flow.** Whenever you roll a Psionic Talent die, you may choose to expend a point of Pneuma to add or subtract 1 to the roll, before determining any changes to your die size resulting from the outcome of the roll. The new value must be a possible roll of the dice. For example, you may turn a d4 roll of 2 into a 1, or a d6 roll of 6 into a 5.
