# Breaking Objects
*Source: Player's Handbook (2024) p. 362. Available in the <span title='Systems Reference Document (5.2)'>SRD</span> and the Free Rules (2024)* 

Objects can be harmed by attacks and by some spells, using the rules below. If an object is exceedingly fragile, the DM may allow a creature to break it automatically with the [Attack](rules/5e/actions.md#Attack) or [Utilize](rules/5e/actions.md#Utilize) action.

## Hit Points

An object is destroyed when it has 0 Hit Points. The Object Hit Points table suggests [Hit Points](rules/5e/variant-rules/hit-points-xphb.md) for fragile and resilient objects that are Large or smaller. To track Hit Points for a Huge or Gargantuan object, divide it into Large or smaller sections, and track each section's [Hit Points](rules/5e/variant-rules/hit-points-xphb.md) separately. The DM determines whether destroying part of an object causes the whole thing to collapse.

**Object Hit Points**

| Size | Fragile | Resilient |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Tiny (bottle, lock) | 2 (`dice:1d4\|noform\|noparens\|avg` (`d4`)) | 5 (`dice:2d4\|noform\|noparens\|avg` (`2d4`)) |
| Small (chest, lute) | 3 (`dice:1d6\|noform\|noparens\|avg` (`d6`)) | 10 (`dice:3d6\|noform\|noparens\|avg` (`3d6`)) |
| Medium (barrel, chandelier) | 4 (`dice:1d8\|noform\|noparens\|avg` (`d8`)) | 18 (`dice:4d8\|noform\|noparens\|avg` (`4d8`)) |
| Large (cart, dining table) | 5 (`dice:1d10\|noform\|noparens\|avg` (`d10`)) | 27 (`dice:5d10\|noform\|noparens\|avg` (`5d10`)) |

## Damage Types and Objects

Objects have [Immunity](rules/5e/variant-rules/immunity-xphb.md) to Poison and Psychic damage. The DM might decide that some [damage types](rules/5e/variant-rules/damage-types-xphb.md) are more or less effective against an object. For example, Bludgeoning damage works well for smashing things but not for cutting. Paper or cloth objects might have [Vulnerability](rules/5e/variant-rules/vulnerability-xphb.md) to Fire damage.

## Damage Threshold

Big objects, such as castle walls, often have extra resilience represented by a damage threshold.

## No Ability Scores

An object lacks ability scores unless a rule assigns scores to the object. Without ability scores, an object can't make ability checks, and it fails all saving throws.
